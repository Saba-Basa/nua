"A topology on X is a collection T of subsets of X, called open subsets, satisfying

Q5: Can you give a concrete example of X and a subset U ‚äÜ X?

A5: To "declare" a subset open means:

     (1) We start with the power set P(X).
         The power set P(X) is the set of all subsets of X. It gives us the
         full collection of candidates from which we may choose open sets.

     (2) T is chosen as a subset of P(X).
         That means every element U of T is itself a subset of X (U ‚äÜ X).

     (3) Openness is defined by logical equivalence:
               "U is open" ‚áî U ‚àà T.
         This means that being open is not proved from a property of U; it is
         assigned entirely by its membership in T. The definition is purely
         syntactic.

     (4) This becomes an axiom of the structure (X, T).
         A topology must satisfy the standard axioms (‚àÖ and X are open,
         arbitrary unions and finite intersections of open sets are open).
         These axioms ensure that the declared open sets behave in a way that
         supports continuity and limits.

     (5) In this context:
         Declaring U open means that openness is introduced by definition,
         not discovered. We create the topological structure by specifying T.
